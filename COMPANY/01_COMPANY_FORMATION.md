# Company Formation and Legal Entity Selection

## Overview

This document provides a comprehensive guide to selecting and forming the appropriate legal entity for a chocolate manufacturing project in India, specifically tailored for Andhra Pradesh-based PMKSY applicants.

**OFFICIAL** — Business entities in India are governed by the Companies Act 2013, Limited Liability Partnership Act 2008, Partnership Act 1932, and various state-specific laws. Entity selection affects taxation, compliance, liability, fundraising capability, and PMKSY eligibility.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document covers **legal entity formation and selection** from a company law perspective. It does **not** address PMKSY-specific eligibility criteria (see [PMKS-004: Entity Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/04_ENTITY_ELIGIBILITY.md)) or PMKSY applicant qualifications (see [PMKS-003: Applicant Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/03_APPLICANT_ELIGIBILITY.md)).

## Entity Types and Comparison

### Available Entity Forms for Chocolate Manufacturing

| Entity Type | Governing Law | Min. Members | Min. Capital | Liability | Suitability for ₹2 Cr PMKSY Project |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|--------------|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| **Sole Proprietorship** | No specific act; governed by tax and local laws | 1 | No minimum | Unlimited | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — suitable for very small scale; limited for ₹2 Cr project |
| **Partnership Firm** | Partnership Act 1932 | 2 | No minimum | Unlimited / Joint and several | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — risky for high-CapEx projects; limited fundraising |
| **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)** | LLP Act 2008 | 2 | No statutory minimum (₹1 lac recommended) | Limited to contribution | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — flexible; suitable for professional/consulting firms; less common for manufacturing |
| **Private Limited Company (Pvt. Ltd.)** | Companies Act 2013 | 2 | No statutory minimum (₹1 lac recommended) | Limited to shares held | OFFICIAL — most common for ₹2 Cr manufacturing projects; preferred by banks |
| **Public Limited Company** | Companies Act 2013 | 7 | Minimum ₹5 lakh | Limited to shares held | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — overkill for ₹2 Cr project; more suited for larger scale or listed entities |
| **One Person Company (OPC)** | Companies Act 2013 | 1 | No statutory minimum (₹1 lac recommended) | Limited to shares held | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — suitable for single-promoter projects; cannot raise equity easily |
| **Producer Company** | Companies Act 2013 (Section 422-424) | 10 producers | No statutory minimum | Limited | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — specifically for agricultural produce; may be relevant for cocoa procurement focus |
| ** Cooperative Society** | Cooperative Societies Act (state-specific) | 10+ members | Varies by state | Limited to contribution | PROJECT ASSUMPTION — state-specific; slower formation; less preferred by banks |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing unit in Andhra Pradesh under PMKSY, **Private Limited Company** is the strongly recommended entity type. It offers limited liability, easy fundraising, bank preference, and PMKSY compatibility.

## Private Limited Company: Detailed Formation Guide

### Why Private Limited Company is Recommended

| Advantage | Explanation | Status |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Limited liability | Promoter personal assets protected | OFFICIAL |
| Separate legal entity | Company can own assets, sue/be sued | OFFICIAL |
| Easy fundraising | Can raise equity, debt; bank preference | BANK PRACTICE |
| Perpetual succession | Continuity beyond promoters | OFFICIAL |
| Tax benefits | Corporate tax rates, deductions | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| PMKSY preference | Most implementing agencies familiar with Pvt. Ltd. structure | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |

### Step-by-Step Registration Process

**OFFICIAL** — Registration through Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) portal:

| Step | Action | Authority | Time | Status |
|------|--------|-----------|------|--------|
| 1 | Obtain Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for directors | Certifying Authority | 1-2 days | OFFICIAL |
| 2 | Apply for Director Identification Number (DIN) | MCA | Instant / 1 day | OFFICIAL |
| 3 | Reserve company name (RUN / SPICe+ form) | MCA (ROC) | 1-2 days | OFFICIAL |
| 4 | Prepare MOA and AOA | Company secretary / consultant | 1-3 days | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| 5 | File SPICe+ (incorporation) with PAN, TAN, GST, EPF, ESI | MCA | 3-7 days | OFFICIAL |
| 6 | Receive Certificate of Incorporation (COI) | MCA (ROC) | Within 7 days of filing | OFFICIAL |
| 7 | Open current account in company name | Bank | 7-14 days | BANK PRACTICE |
| 8 | Commence business | — | After COI | OFFICIAL |

### Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA)

**OFFICIAL** — MOA defines company's relationship with outside world; AOA governs internal management.

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Object clause in MOA for a chocolate manufacturing company should include:
- Manufacturing, processing, packaging, and sale of chocolate, cocoa products, and confectionery
- Related food processing activities
- Trading in raw materials and finished goods
- Export/import of chocolate products
- Any other activity incidental to the main business

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Ensure MOA object clause is broad enough to cover all planned activities to avoid future amendment requirements.

## Alternative Entity Forms

### Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — LLP combines partnership flexibility with limited liability. Suitable when:
- Promoters are professionals or service providers
- Limited capital requirement
- Flexibility in management desired

**Not recommended for** ₹2 Cr manufacturing because:
- Banks prefer companies for term loans
- Less familiar structure for PMKSY
- Limited ability to raise equity

### Sole Proprietorship / Partnership

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Simplest to form but not recommended for ₹2 Cr chocolate manufacturing because:
- Unlimited personal liability
- Difficult to raise term loans
- PMKSY may require entity registration
- No separate legal status

### One Person Company (OPC)

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Suitable for single-promoter projects. Can convert to Pvt. Ltd. when additional promoters join.

**Limitations**:
- Cannot raise equity from additional shareholders easily
- Mandatory conversion to Pvt. Ltd. if paid-up capital exceeds ₹2 Cr or turnover exceeds ₹200 Cr (not applicable at inception)
- Less familiar to banks for manufacturing

### Producer Company

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Specifically designed for agricultural producers. May be relevant if the chocolate unit is integrated with cocoa farmer procurement. Requires:
- Minimum 10 individual producer members
- Objects aligned with agricultural produce processing
- State-specific registration through Regional Director

## Capital Structure and Promoter Composition

### Authorized vs. Paid-Up Capital

| Term | Definition | Requirement | Status |
|------|------------|-------------|--------|
| Authorized Capital | Maximum capital company can raise | Set in MOA; can be increased | OFFICIAL |
| Paid-Up Capital | Capital actually contributed by promoters | Minimum recommended ₹1 lac for manufacturing | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Subscribed Capital | Portion of authorized capital subscribed | Should cover promoter contribution + bank loan | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — For ₹2 crore project:
- Authorized Capital: ₹5-10 lakh (allows room for expansion)
- Paid-Up Capital: At least ₹1-2 lakh (demonstrates commitment)
- Promoter Contribution: Typically 25-35% of TPC (₹50 lakh - ₹70 lakh)

### Promoter Composition

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Typical promoter composition for ₹2 crore chocolate project:

| Promoter Type | Typical Shareholding | Role |
|--------------|----------------------|------|
| Anchor / Lead Promoter | 40-60% | Strategic direction, majority control |
| Technical Promoter | 10-20% | Process expertise, operations |
| Financial Promoter | 10-20% | Funding, banking relationships |
| Operational Partner | 5-15% | Day-to-day management |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Ensure promoters collectively bring:
- Industry knowledge (food processing / chocolate)
- Financial capacity (net worth, liquidity)
- Technical expertise (or engagement of qualified consultants)
- Market access / commercial capability

## Director and Officer Requirements

### Board Composition for Private Limited Company

**OFFICIAL** — Minimum requirements under Companies Act 2013:

| Requirement | Detail | Status |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Minimum directors | 2 | OFFICIAL |
| Maximum directors | 15 (can increase to 21 by special resolution) | OFFICIAL |
| Resident director | At least 1 director who has stayed in India ≥182 days in preceding calendar year | OFFICIAL |
| Woman director | Not mandatory for private companies (mandatory for listed and certain public companies) | OFFICIAL |
| Independent director | Not mandatory for private companies | OFFICIAL |

### Key Officer Positions

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing Pvt. Ltd., recommended officer structure:

| Position | Requirement | Status |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Managing Director (MD) / Whole-time Director | Recommended for active management | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Chief Financial Officer (CFO) | Recommended if turnover exceeds threshold; advisable for bank preference | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Company Secretary | Mandatory only if paid-up capital ≥₹10 Cr (not applicable); advisable but not mandatory | OFFICIAL — under Companies Act 2013 Section 203 for private companies with paid-up capital below ₹10 Cr |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm if paid-up capital thresholds for mandatory CFO/CS have been updated in current Companies Act rules.

## Statutory Registers and Records

### Mandatory Registers

**OFFICIAL** — Every company must maintain:

| Register | Content | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Register of Members | Shareholder details | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Directors | Director particulars | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Charges | Charges created on company assets | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Loans/Investments | Loans given and investments made | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Contracts with Related Parties | Related party transactions | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Deposits | Deposits accepted from public (if applicable) | OFFICIAL |
| Register of Employees | Employee details | OFFICIAL |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Maintain registers in digital format with backup; ensure statutory audit covers all registers.

### Board Minutes and Resolutions

**OFFICIAL** — Board meetings and resolutions must be minuted and filed as required.

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore project:
- Board resolutions for: project approval, loan sanction, bank account opening, security creation, asset purchase above threshold
- Minutes must be recorded, signed, and maintained in minute books

## Tax Implications by Entity Type

### Tax Rate Comparison

| Entity Type | Tax Regime | Key Considerations | Status |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Proprietorship | Individual tax slabs (up to 42%) | Personal tax; no corporate tax | OFFICIAL |
| Partnership | 30% corporate tax + surcharge/cess | Pass-through to partners; dividend distribution not applicable | OFFICIAL |
| LLP | 30% corporate tax + surcharge/cess | Similar to partnership; MAT may apply | OFFICIAL |
| Private Limited Company | 25% (domestic company with turnover <₹400 Cr) or 30% | 25% rate applies if turnover <₹400 Cr; surcharge/cess additional; dividend distribution tax history changed; new tax regime may apply | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Current corporate tax rates and new tax regime applicability for FY 2026-27. Consult CA for exact liability.

### GST Implications

**OFFICIAL** — Chocolate manufacturing falls under GST with appropriate HSN codes. Entity type does not determine GST applicability; turnover and activity type do.

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — GST registration based on:
- Mandatory if turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for special category states)
- Mandatory for inter-state supply irrespective of turnover
- Composition scheme typically not available for manufacturing

## Conversion and Restructuring

### Converting Entity Type After PMKSY Sanction

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Changing entity structure after PMKSY sanction may require:
- Implementing agency approval
- Amendment of PMKSY agreement
- Reappraisal of project
- Possible change in promoter contribution requirements

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — It is advisable to finalize entity type before PMKSY application to avoid post-sanction restructuring complications.

### Amalgamation, Merger, Acquisition

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Not typically relevant for initial ₹2 crore project. Consider only at expansion or scale-up stage.
