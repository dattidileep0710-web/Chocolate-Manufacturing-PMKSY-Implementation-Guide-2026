# Applicant Eligibility

## Purpose of Applicant Eligibility

Applicant eligibility determines **who can apply** for PMKSY assistance. It assesses the qualifications, capabilities, and track record of the person or group seeking support, independent of the project’s technical merits or land availability.

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY applicants must satisfy eligibility criteria established by the implementing agency. The applicant is the Project Implementation Agency (PIA) that will execute the project and receive subsidy.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document addresses **applicant eligibility** — the human/organizational qualifications of the party seeking scheme support. It does **not** address:
> - Project eligibility (see [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Entity eligibility (see [PMKS-004: Entity Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/04_ENTITY_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Land eligibility (see [PMKS-005: Land Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/05_LAND_ELIGIBILITY.md))

## Who Can Apply Under PMKSY

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY eligibility extends to a broad range of applicants in the private and public sectors. The scheme is designed to encourage entrepreneurship, attract private investment, and leverage institutional capability.

### Applicant Categories

| Category | Examples | Typical Eligibility Status |
|----------|----------|---------------------------|
| Individual entrepreneurs | Proprietors, self-employed | Eligible, subject to capability criteria |
| Proprietorship firms | Single-owner businesses | Eligible |
| Partnership firms | General/Limited Liability Partnerships | Eligible |
| Private limited companies | Incorporated under Companies Act | Eligible |
| Public limited companies | Listed/unlisted public companies | Eligible |
| Cooperative societies | Registered cooperatives | Eligible |
| Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) | FPOs with valid registration | Eligible |
| Self-Help Groups (SHGs) | Registered SHGs | Eligible |
| Public sector entities | Government departments, PSUs | Eligible if 51%+ government stake |
| Joint ventures | Domestic/international JV | Eligible if promoter criteria met |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing unit in Andhra Pradesh, the most likely applicant categories are:
- Private limited company (most common for this scale)
- Proprietorship firm (simpler structure, smaller promoters)
- Partnership firm (multiple promoters)

## Promoter/Applicant Qualifications

### General Qualifications

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY requires promoters to demonstrate basic suitability. Typical criteria include:

| Qualification | Requirement | Status |
|---------------|-------------|--------|
| Age | Minimum 18 years | OFFICIAL — contracts/agreements legal requirement |
| Citizenship | Indian citizen / OCI / registered entity in India | OFFICIAL — PMKSY is a central government scheme for Indian entities |
| Mental capacity | Competent to contract | OFFICIAL — legal requirement |
| Legal standing | Not insolvent / undischarged insolvent | OFFICIAL — legal disqualification |

### Technical Qualification

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Promoters are expected to possess or acquire:

| Requirement | Description | Verification Status |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Technical know-how | Understanding of chocolate manufacturing process | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Industry experience | Prior experience in food processing or relevant industry | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Technical team | Qualified personnel or technical associate | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Technology partner | OEM/technology provider if promoter lacks expertise | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| DPR preparation | Competent consultant or in-house capability | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — For a first-time chocolate manufacturing project, promoters should engage:
- A food technology consultant for process design
- An equipment OEM for technology selection
- A financial consultant for DPR preparation and bank liaison

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI specifies minimum promoter experience years or technical qualifications for food processing projects.

### Financial Qualification

**OFFICIAL** — Promoters must demonstrate financial capacity:

| Requirement | Description | Status |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Promoter contribution | Minimum equity investment in project | OFFICIAL — scheme requirement, exact percentage REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |
| Net worth | Adequate net worth to support project | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |
| Source of funds | Documentary evidence for own funds | OFFICIAL — standard banking requirement |
| Creditworthiness | No adverse credit history | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm minimum promoter contribution percentage under current-year PMKSY guidelines (typically 25% for general areas). Verify net worth requirements.

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Verify whether MoFPI or implementing agencies specify minimum liquid net worth or tangible net worth.

### Track Record

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Promoters are evaluated based on:

| Track Record Element | Relevance | Status |
|----------------------|-----------|--------|
| Previous project execution | Demonstrates execution capability | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Repayment history | Credit discipline | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Industry experience | Domain knowledge | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Legal compliance | Clean regulatory record | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI disqualifies promoters with past default records or criminal convictions.

## Applicant Disqualifications

### General Disqualifications

**OFFICIAL** — Applicants are disqualified if:

| Disqualification Reason | Basis | Status |
|-------------------------|-------|--------|
| Unsound mind / insolvent | Legal incapacity | OFFICIAL — Indian Contract Act |
| Undischarged insolvent | Legal disqualification | OFFICIAL — Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code |
| Conviction for moral turpitude | Criminal disqualification | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |
| Government dues outstanding | Financial disqualification | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |
| Previous scheme default | Recovery of dues | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |

### PMKSY-Specific Disqualifications

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Specific disqualifications under PMKSY may include:
- Ineligibility under terms of earlier PMKSY sanctions
- Diversion of earlier PMKSY funds
- Non-completion of earlier PMKSY projects
- Misrepresentation in earlier applications

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Applicants with pending PMKSY projects may need to complete existing commitments before new applications.

### Chocolate Manufacturing-Specific Disqualifications

## Relationship with DPR Preparation

### Who Can Prepare the DPR

**OFFICIAL** — The DPR must be prepared by a competent entity. Acceptable preparers include:

1. **Applicant’s own team**: If technically qualified personnel available
2. **Private technical consultants**: Registered consultants with food processing experience
3. **Bank-appointed consultants**: When bank finances the project
4. **Implementing agency consultants**: If agency provides this service
5. **Institutional consultants**: CFTC, IIFPT, or other MoFPI-recognized institutions

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing project, engage a consultant with:
- Food processing industry experience
- PMKSY DPR template familiarity
- Chocolate/confectionery project experience (preferred)

### Applicant’s Role in DPR

| Responsibility | Description | Status |
|----------------|-------------|--------|
| Providing data | Project details, financial information, promoter background | OFFICIAL — applicant must provide accurate information |
| Site access | Enabling site visits and surveys | OFFICIAL — standard requirement |
| Cost verification | Validating cost estimates | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Approval authority | Finalizing and signing DPR | OFFICIAL — applicant must endorse DPR |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI mandates specific consultant qualifications or DPR preparation templates.

See [PMKS-007: Required Documents](PMKSY_MASTER/07_REQUIRED_DOCUMENTS.md) for DPR format requirements.

## Relationship with Bank Loan Appraisal

### Applicant-Bank Relationship

**BANK PRACTICE** — The applicant’s eligibility is as important to the bank as to MoFPI:

| Bank Criterion | Applicant Relevance |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Creditworthiness | Assessed via CIBIL score, financial statements |
| Net worth | Must support promoter contribution |
| Experience | Domain experience reduces risk |
| Management capability | Team’s ability to execute |
| Collateral availability | Security for term loan |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Even if PMKSY subsidy reduces the promoter contribution burden, banks will appraise the full project without subsidy adjustment for eligibility purposes.

### PMKSY-Bank Coordination

**BANK PRACTICE** — Some banks require MoFPI pre-approval before sanctioning the project. Applicants should:
- Apply to MoFPI parallel to bank appraisal
- Share PMKSY guidelines with bank
- Provide MoFPI in-principle approval to bank when available

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether all banks accept MoFPI in-principle approval, or if specific banks have additional requirements.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md) for detailed bank appraisal procedures.

## Relationship with Subsidy Processing

### Applicant’s Responsibilities

**BANK PRACTICE** — The applicant bears responsibility for:

| Responsibility | Stage | Description |
|----------------|-------|-------------|
| Application accuracy | Pre-sanction | Complete and truthful information |
| Document submission | Pre-sanction | All required documents |
| Expenditure certification | During implementation | Proof of expenditure for subsidy release |
| Project monitoring | During implementation | Progress reporting to bank and MoFPI |
| Completion certification | Post-completion | Final project completion report |

### Applicant Obligations

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Applicants may be required to:
- Execute agreement with implementing agency
- Maintain separate escrow/bank account for subsidy funds
- Submit periodic utilization certificates
- Allow inspection by MoFPI/bank officials
- Maintain asset and project records for audit period

**BANK PRACTICE** — Banks typically:
- Open separate accounts for project implementation
- Release loan installments based on utilization certificates
- Coordinate with MoFPI for subsidy disbursement
- Monitor project progress through site visits

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact applicant obligations under current-year PMKSY agreement.

See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md) for subsidy processing details.

## Common Applicant Eligibility Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Misrepresenting Promoter Experience
- **Error**: Inflating or fabricating technical/financial experience
- **Prevention**: Provide accurate, verifiable experience records; third-party validation preferred

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Track Record
- **Error**: Applying with promoters who have default history
- **Prevention**: Disclose all material facts; address adverse history proactively

### Mistake 3: Wrong Applicant Category
- **Error**: Applying as individual when project scale requires company structure
- **Prevention**: Choose appropriate structure; consult legal/financial advisor early

### Mistake 4: Multiple Applications
- **Error**: Submitting multiple applications for same/similar projects
- **Prevention**: Consolidate into single, comprehensive application

### Mistake 5: Inadequate Financial Documentation
- **Error**: Supporting net worth claims with insufficient documentation
- **Prevention**: Maintain audited financials for at least 3 years; source all funds

### Mistake 6: DPR Delegation Without Oversight
- **Error**: Leaving DPR entirely to consultant without review
- **Prevention**: Actively participate in DPR preparation; understand all assumptions

### Mistake 7: Underestimating PMKSY Obligations
- **Error**: Treating subsidy as free grant without compliance requirements
- **Prevention**: Understand ongoing reporting, audit, and compliance obligations
