# Land Eligibility

## Purpose of Land Eligibility

Land eligibility determines whether the **land on which a chocolate manufacturing project is proposed** satisfies PMKSY requirements. This document establishes land-related criteria that must be met independent of project design, applicant qualifications, or entity structure.

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY requires that projects be implemented on eligible land with clear title, appropriate land-use classification, and requisite approvals.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document addresses **land eligibility only**. It does **not** address:
> - Project eligibility (see [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Applicant eligibility (see [PMKS-003: Applicant Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/03_APPLICANT_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Entity eligibility (see [PMKS-004: Entity Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/04_ENTITY_ELIGIBILITY.md))

---

## Importance of Land Eligibility

**OFFICIAL** — Land eligibility is a critical gating factor because:
- PMKSY subsidy is contingent on project implementation on eligible land
- Land title and land-use clearance must precede project sanction
- Land-related disqualifications are often irreversible

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing unit in Andhra Pradesh, land eligibility verification should begin at project conception and conclude before DPR finalization.

## Land Ownership Eligibility

### Eligible Ownership Types

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY recognizes the following land ownership/control patterns:

| Ownership Type | Eligibility Status | Verification Required |
|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Freehold ownership | Eligible | Title deed, revenue records |
| Leasehold (≥30 years) | Eligible | Lease agreement, lessor NOC |
| Leasehold (10-30 years) | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Lease agreement, renewal terms |
| Land allotment by government | Eligible | Allotment letter, possession letter |
| Joint ownership | Eligible | All co-owners' consent |
| Land owned by related party | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Arm's-length pricing, declaration |
| Land owned by entity/promoter group | Eligible | Title verification |
| Land under mortgage | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Bank NOC for project land |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a chocolate manufacturing unit, freehold or long-term leasehold (≥30 years) is strongly preferred to satisfy bank and MoFPI requirements.

### Title Verification Requirements

**OFFICIAL** — Applicants must establish clear, marketable title through:

| Document | Purpose | Status |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Title deed / Sale deed | Proof of ownership | OFFICIAL — primary evidence |
| Encumbrance certificate | No liens/claims on land | OFFICIAL — typically 13-30 years |
| Revenue records / 7/12 extract | Revenue department records | OFFICIAL — Maharashtra/Gujarat model |
| Property tax receipt | Current tax status | OFFICIAL — municipal/panchayat |
| Survey number / Plot number | Identification | OFFICIAL — revenue requirement |
| Khata / Property card | Municipal records | OFFICIAL — where applicable |
| Mutation entry | Transfer recorded in revenue records | OFFICIAL — post-purchase requirement |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Obtain a property lawyer's opinion on title before proceeding. For bank-financed projects, the bank will independently verify title.

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI specifies a minimum title verification period (e.g., 13-year encumbrance certificate).

## Land-Use Classification

### Eligible Land-Use Classifications

**OFFICIAL** — Land must have appropriate zoning for industrial/food processing use.

| Land-Use Classification | Eligibility | Notes |
|-------------------------|-------------|-------|
| Industrial (as per local master plan) | Eligible | Preferred classification |
| Food processing zone / food park | Eligible | Often has additional infrastructure support |
| Agricultural (with conversion) | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Conversion to industrial required |
| Mixed-use (industrial permitted) | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Verify specific permissions |
| Commercial | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | May require additional clearances |
| Residential | Not eligible | Conversion to industrial required |

**OFFICIAL** — Land-use conversion from agricultural to industrial is governed by:
- State Town and Country Planning Act
- Local development plan / master plan
- Revenue department conversion orders
- AP Industrial Policy provisions (for Andhra Pradesh)

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For Andhra Pradesh, land-use conversion follows AP Land Revenue Act and relevant CCMC/DGCCI notifications.

### Land-Use Conversion Process

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Typical conversion process:

1. Application to local planning authority
2. No-objection from revenue department
3. [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] — Environmental impact assessment if applicable
4. Conversion order / change of land-use certificate
5. Mutation in revenue records

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Timeline for land-use conversion varies by state; confirm AP-specific timelines.

## Minimum Land Requirements

### Area Considerations

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Minimum land requirements depend on project scale, layout, and future expansion:

| Project Scale | Assumed Minimum Land | Rationale |
|---------------|----------------------|-----------|
| ₹2 crore chocolate unit | 2,000 - 5,000 sq.m | Factory, utilities, parking, future expansion |
| With cocoa primary processing | 5,000 - 10,000 sq.m | Additional bean storage, drying, primary processing |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Acquire land with 30-50% excess area to accommodate:
- Future expansion (Phase 2)
- Utility infrastructure
- Internal roads and circulation
- Green buffer / environmental compliance
- Parking and logistics

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI or implementing agencies specify minimum land area requirements for food processing projects.

### Layout Considerations

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Land should accommodate:

| Zone | Required Area | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Processing building | 40-50% | Main production hall |
| Warehouse / storage | 20-25% | Raw material and finished goods |
| Utility block | 10-15% | Power, DG, boiler, compressor |
| Laboratory / QC | 5-8% | Quality testing |
| Admin / offices | 5-8% | Management, accounts, security |
| Open area / future expansion | 15-25% | Staging, future phases |

## Location Eligibility

### Geographic Eligibility

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY applies nationwide. For Andhra Pradesh:

| Region | Subsidy Classification | Status |
|--------|------------------------|--------|
| Andhra Pradesh (most districts) | General Area | OFFICIAL — not NE, Himalayan, Island, or ITDP |
| ITDP-designated areas in AP | Difficult Area | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION — verify specific district classification |
| Coastal AP | General Area | OFFICIAL based on current ITDP list |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Land in industrial areas / food parks / MSME clusters in Andhra Pradesh is preferred for:
- Infrastructure availability (power, water, roads)
- Common facilities (effluent treatment, testing labs)
- Single-window clearances

### Excluded Locations

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Land in the following locations may be excluded or require additional clearances:

| Location Type | Status | Requirement |
|---------------|--------|-------------|
| Residential areas | Not eligible | Land-use conversion required |
| Ecologically sensitive zones | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Environmental clearance |
| Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | CRZ clearance from MoEFCC |
| Industrial riverfronts (polluted) | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Environmental compliance |
| Heritage zones | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Heritage committee clearance |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether MoFPI excludes specific locations or land types for food processing projects.

## Environmental and Regulatory Clearances

### Clearances Linked to Land

**OFFICIAL** — The following clearances are land/environment-dependent:

| Clearance | Authority | When Required | Status |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Environmental clearance | MoEFCC / SPCB | Based on project scale and category | OFFICIAL — under EIA Notification |
| Consent to establish | SPCB Andhra Pradesh | Before construction | OFFICIAL — under Air/Water Acts |
| Hazardous waste authorization | SPCB | If hazardous waste generated | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION — chocolate manufacturing typically generates minimal hazardous waste |
| Fire safety NOC | Fire department | Before commissioning | OFFICIAL — state Fire Services Act |
| Building plan approval | Local municipal body | Before construction | OFFICIAL — local building bylaws |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate unit in Andhra Pradesh, environmental clearance under Category B (state-level) or general conditions may apply depending on capacity and location.

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact environmental categorization for chocolate manufacturing under MoEFCC EIA Notification 2006 (as amended).

### Land-Specific Environmental Concerns

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Verify:
- Distance from residential areas (buffers)
- Ground water availability and extraction permissions
- Effluent disposal connectivity (common ETP or project ETP)
- Solid waste disposal arrangements
- Noise and air emission zones

See [PMKS-013: Compliance](PMKSY_MASTER/13_COMPLIANCE.md) for comprehensive compliance requirements.

## Relationship with Project Eligibility

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Land eligibility is a prerequisite for project eligibility under PMKSY:

| Project Eligibility Condition | Land Requirement |
|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Location in eligible area | Confirm AP classification |
| Minimum land area | Site-specific verification |
| Land-use compatibility | Industrial/food processing use |
| Title clarity | No disputes or encumbrances |
| Environmental feasibility | Clearances obtainable |

**OFFICIAL** — A project on ineligible land is disqualified regardless of other merits.

## Relationship with DPR Preparation

### Land Details in DPR

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — The DPR must contain:

| DPR Section | Land Information Required |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Executive summary | Location, area, ownership summary |
| Project description | Site address, survey numbers, boundaries |
| Land details | Area, ownership, land-use, layout plan |
| Site suitability | Topography, soil, drainage, access |
| Clearances | Environmental, land-use, building approvals |
| Financial feasibility | Land cost, development cost |

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Attach the following to DPR:
- Latest survey plan / plot plan
- Revenue records / 7-12 extract
- Encumbrance certificate
- Land-use conversion order (if applicable)
- Location map showing access roads, nearby facilities

See [PMKS-007: Required Documents](PMKSY_MASTER/07_REQUIRED_DOCUMENTS.md) for complete DPR requirements.

## Relationship with Bank Appraisal

### Bank Land Verification

**BANK PRACTICE** — Banks independently verify land eligibility as part of project appraisal:

| Bank Check | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| Title search and legal opinion | Ensure marketable title |
| Encumbrance verification | No undisclosed liabilities |
| Land-use verification | Confirm zoning compliance |
| Valuation | Collateral assessment for term loan |
| Technical feasibility | Site suitability for project |

**BANK PRACTICE** — For term loans secured by land mortgage:
- Land must be in applicant's name or lease in applicant's favor
- No prior encumbrances unless bank's prior charge is acceptable
- Fair market value determined by bank-approved valuer

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Banks will not reduce loan quantum due to PMKSY subsidy but will verify land eligibility independently.

### Land as Collateral

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore project, land typically serves as primary collateral:
- Bank evaluates land value alongside project viability
- Loan-to-value ratio typically 50-70% of land value
- PMKSY subsidy does not affect collateral requirements

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether banks accept MoFPI in-principle approval conditional on land verification.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md) for bank appraisal procedures.

## Common Land Eligibility Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Assuming Land Ownership Equals Eligibility
- **Error**: Believing that owning land automatically satisfies land eligibility
- **Prevention**: Verify title, land-use, and all clearances independently

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Land-Use Conversion Requirements
- **Error**: Proceeding with agricultural land without conversion order
- **Prevention**: Obtain conversion order before DPR finalization

### Mistake 3: Inadequate Title Verification
- **Error**: Relying on seller's representation without independent verification
- **Prevention**: Obtain encumbrance certificate covering minimum 13-30 years

### Mistake 4: Underestimating Land Size Requirements
- **Error**: Acquiring minimum land without buffer for expansion
- **Prevention**: Acquire 30-50% excess land for future phases

### Mistake 5: Overlooking Environmental Constraints
- **Error**: Acquiring land without checking CRZ, eco-sensitive zones, or industrial restrictions
- **Prevention**: Verify environmental constraints at land selection stage

### Mistake 6: Lease Agreement Gaps
- **Error**: Lease terms insufficient for project duration or bank requirements
- **Prevention**: Ensure lease term ≥ project tenure + security period; include renewal options

### Mistake 7: Missing Government Approvals
- **Error**: Assuming industrial area = all clearances in place
- **Prevention**: Obtain NOC from relevant authorities; do not rely on general area approvals

## Decision Checklist: Is the Land Eligible?

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Use this checklist to assess land eligibility:

| Check | Status | Required Action if FAIL |
|--------|--------|------------------------|
| Land title clear and marketable | [ ] | Obtain legal opinion; resolve encumbrances |
| Land-use classification is industrial/food processing | [ ] | Obtain conversion order or choose alternate site |
| Minimum area adequate for current + future needs | [ ] | Acquire additional land or reduce project scope |
| Andhra Pradesh location in eligible zone | [ ] | Verify ITDP/CRZ/eco-sensitive restrictions |
| No prior commitments/liens on land | [ ] | Obtain clearance from prior lenders |
| Environmental clearances feasible | [ ] | Engage consultant for scoping |
| Land can be mortgaged to bank (if required) | [ ] | Verify title acceptability to bank |
| Lease term adequate (if applicable) | [ ] | Negotiate longer lease with renewal option |
| Access road and infrastructure available | [ ] | Verify with local authorities |
