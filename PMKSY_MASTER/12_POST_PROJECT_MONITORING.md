# Post-Project Monitoring

## Purpose of Post-Project Monitoring

Post-project monitoring ensures that a sanctioned PMKSY chocolate manufacturing project achieves its intended objectives, remains compliant, and continues to deliver value after subsidy release. It protects public investment by verifying asset utilization, production performance, and ongoing compliance.

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY mandates post-project monitoring to ensure that subsidy-assisted assets are used for the stated purpose for the agreed duration.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document addresses **post-project monitoring only** — activities after final grant release. It does **not** address:
> - Pre-sanction appraisal (see [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md))
> - Grant release mechanics (see [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md))
> - Compliance requirements (see [PMKS-013: Compliance](PMKSY_MASTER/13_COMPLIANCE.md))

## Monitoring Objectives

### Primary Objectives

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY post-project monitoring aims to:

1. **Asset Verification**: Confirm that subsidized assets exist and are in operational condition
2. **Utilization Tracking**: Verify that assets are used for the intended purpose
3. **Production Verification**: Confirm actual production against projected capacity
4. **Financial Health**: Assess ongoing financial viability and loan repayment
5. **Compliance Continuity**: Ensure continued adherence to statutory requirements
6. **Employment Generation**: Track employment outcomes (if project conditions included)
7. **Value Addition**: Verify ongoing value addition activities
8. **Scheme Integrity**: Prevent diversion, sale, or unauthorized use of subsidized assets

### Monitoring Principles

| Principle | Description | Status |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Non-intrusive | Monitoring should not disrupt operations | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Risk-based | Focus on higher-risk projects | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Cooperative | Collaborative approach with applicant | PROJECT ASSUMPTION |
| Evidence-based | Conclusions drawn from documented evidence | OFFICIAL |
| Time-bound | Defined monitoring period and frequency | REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Capacity utilization is monitored to ensure subsidy objectives are met:

| Utilization Level | Interpretation | Typical Action |

## Common Monitoring Issues

### Issue 1: Incomplete Self-Reporting
- **Problem**: Applicant fails to submit timely or complete reports
- **Impact**: IA cannot assess project status; delays in issue identification
- **Prevention**: IA sets up automated reminders; applicant assigns dedicated monitoring officer

### Issue 2: Asset Misuse
- **Problem**: Subsidized assets used for purposes other than approved project
- **Impact**: Breach of sanction terms; recovery risk
- **Prevention**: Clear asset marking; periodic physical verification

### Issue 3: Underutilization Without Explanation
- **Problem**: Low capacity utilization with no documented reason
- **Impact**: Monitoring flags concern; potential escalation
- **Prevention**: Early warning system; IA engagement when utilization drops below 60%

### Issue 4: Financial Distress Unreported
- **Problem**: Applicant experiencing financial stress but not reporting
- **Impact**: Loan default risk; project failure
- **Prevention**: Bank shares financial stress signals with IA; applicant encouraged to report proactively

### Issue 5: Compliance Lapses
- **Problem**: Licenses, clearances, or tax payments lapse during operations
- **Impact**: Regulatory penalties; project shutdown risk
- **Prevention**: Compliance calendar; automated renewal reminders

### Issue 6: Ghost Projects
- **Problem**: Project exists on paper but not in physical form
- **Impact**: Severe fraud; recovery and blacklisting
- **Prevention**: Rigorous pre-sanction due diligence; surprise inspections

### Issue 7: Expansion Without Approval
- **Problem**: Applicant expands or diversifies project beyond sanctioned scope
- **Impact**: Breach of sanction terms; subsidy at risk
- **Prevention**: Clear communication of approval boundaries; prior approval required for changes
