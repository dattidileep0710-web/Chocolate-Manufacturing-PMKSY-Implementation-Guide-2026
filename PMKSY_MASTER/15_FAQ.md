# FAQ

## Overview

This FAQ provides quick-reference answers to the most frequently asked questions about PMKSY for chocolate manufacturing projects in Andhra Pradesh. Every answer is based exclusively on PMKS-001 through PMKS-014 and directs readers to the authoritative source document for full details.

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Use this FAQ for rapid orientation. For detailed requirements, always refer to the linked PMKSY master documents.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document provides **concise answers** only. It does **not** replace detailed guidance in PMKS-001 through PMKS-014.

## PMKSY Overview

**Q1: What is PMKSY?**

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana) is a central government scheme for integrated development of the food processing sector, with a total outlay of ₹6,000 crore (2016-2025-26). See [PMKS-001: Scheme Overview](PMKSY_MASTER/01_SCHEME_OVERVIEW.md).

**Q2: What is the objective of PMKSY?**

**OFFICIAL** — Objectives include: increasing food processing to 25%, reducing post-harvest losses to 5-10%, creating 80 lakh jobs by 2026, and boosting exports. See [PMKS-001: Scheme Overview](PMKSY_MASTER/01_SCHEME_OVERVIEW.md).

**Q3: Which ministry administers PMKSY?**

**OFFICIAL** — Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) is the nodal ministry. See [PMKS-001: Scheme Overview](PMKSY_MASTER/01_SCHEME_OVERVIEW.md) and [PMKS-009: Implementing Agencies](PMKSY_MASTER/09_IMPLEMENTING_AGENCIES.md).

**Q4: What are the main PMKSY components?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — PMKSY includes: (1) Integrated Cold Chain, (2) Creation/Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities, (3) Meat Processing, (4) Primary Processing Infrastructure. Chocolate manufacturing typically falls under Component 2. See [PMKS-001: Scheme Overview](PMKSY_MASTER/01_SCHEME_OVERVIEW.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm current component structure and applicability for chocolate manufacturing with MoFPI.

**Q5: Is PMKSY applicable to chocolate manufacturing?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Yes, chocolate manufacturing is classified under food processing and may qualify under Component 2 (Creation/Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities), provided it meets project eligibility criteria. See [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm chocolate-specific treatment under PMKSY with MoFPI.

## Eligible Project Cost

**Q25: What costs are eligible for PMKSY subsidy?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Eligible costs typically include: land development, civil works (factory, warehouse, lab), plant and machinery, electrical and utilities, laboratory equipment, erection and commissioning, DPR/consultancy (within limits), and pre-operative expenses (within limits).

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact eligible cost categories and ceilings with MoFPI guidelines.

**Q26: Is working capital eligible for subsidy?**

**OFFICIAL** — No, working capital is ineligible for PMKSY subsidy. PMKSY is a capital subsidy scheme. Working capital must be arranged separately through bank or own funds.

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**Q27: Is the cost of land acquisition eligible?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Land cost is often excluded or capped under PMKSY. Many food processing schemes exclude raw land value from Eligible Project Cost (EPC). Confirm with implementing agency.

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**Q28: Are there cost ceilings for specific items (civil, lab, consultancy)?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — PMKSY typically imposes ceilings on cost categories (e.g., per sq.m civil cost, consultancy as % of EPC, pre-operative expenses as % of EPC). Confirm exact norms from current MoFPI guidelines.

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**Q29: How is the subsidy amount calculated?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Subsidy = Eligible Project Cost (EPC) × Subsidy Rate %. The rate varies by area type (General Areas vs. Difficult Areas). Exact rates must be confirmed with MoFPI.

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md) and [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact subsidy rates and maximum ceilings.

## Application Workflow

**Q35: What is the PMKSY application process?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — The process involves: (1) Project preparation, (2) DPR preparation, (3) Bank appraisal (parallel), (4) PMKSY application submission, (5) IA appraisal, (6) Sanction, (7) Implementation, (8) Grant release, (9) Post-project monitoring.

See [PMKS-008: Application Workflow](PMKSY_MASTER/08_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md).

**Q36: How long does the PMKSY application process take?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Typical timelines: DPR preparation 4-8 weeks, bank appraisal 4-8 weeks, IA appraisal 4-12 weeks, sanction 2-4 weeks. Total: approximately 14-32 weeks from application to sanction. Confirm current timelines with implementing agency.

See [PMKS-008: Application Workflow](PMKSY_MASTER/08_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md).

**Q37: Should I approach the bank before or after PMKSY application?**

**BANK PRACTICE** — Run bank appraisal in parallel with PMKSY application. Some banks may require MoFPI in-principle approval before final sanction. Do not wait for PMKSY sanction before approaching the bank.

See [PMKS-008: Application Workflow](PMKSY_MASTER/08_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md) and [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md).

**Q38: How do I track my PMKSY application status?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Track through the implementing agency's online portal (if available), or maintain regular contact with the IA nodal officer. Monitor weekly during appraisal phase.

See [PMKS-008: Application Workflow](PMKSY_MASTER/08_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md) and [PMKS-009: Implementing Agencies](PMKSY_MASTER/09_IMPLEMENTING_AGENCIES.md).

**Q39: What happens after PMKSY sanction?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — After sanction: execute PMKSY agreement, obtain bank term loan sanction, complete statutory clearances, commence implementation, and coordinate with IA and bank for grant release.

See [PMKS-008: Application Workflow](PMKSY_MASTER/08_APPLICATION_WORKFLOW.md) and [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md).

## Project Appraisal

**Q44: What does PMKSY appraisal check?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — PMKSY appraisal verifies: eligibility (project, applicant, entity, land), technical feasibility (process, equipment, capacity), financial viability (costs, means of finance, projections), and compliance readiness.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md).

**Q45: Does PMKSY appraisal affect my bank loan?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — PMKSY and bank appraisals are independent but complementary. Bank appraises at 100% TPC without deducting PMKSY subsidy. Both must be favorable for project success.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md).

**Q46: What financial ratios are checked during appraisal?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Typical ratios: Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) >1.5x, Internal Rate of Return (IRR) >15%, payback period <7 years, break-even capacity <70% utilization, debt-equity ratio <2:1. Confirm exact thresholds with bank/IA.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md).

**Q47: Can I get PMKSY subsidy without a bank loan?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — PMKSY requires promoters to contribute minimum equity (typically 25%). The balance can be through bank loan or other debt. While a bank loan is not strictly mandatory, most projects require debt financing to be viable.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md) and [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether 100% equity-funded projects are eligible.

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Subsidy is released in installments tied to milestones: after equipment supply/civil progress (first release), after installation/commissioning (second release), and after trial production/commercial operation (final release).

See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact milestone definitions and timing.

**OFFICIAL** — Required documents include: Utilization Certificate (UC) from CA, expenditure proof (invoices/bills), progress report, site inspection report, bank certificate, no-due certificate, and quality assurance certificates (as applicable).

See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md) and [PMKS-007: Required Documents](PMKSY_MASTER/07_REQUIRED_DOCUMENTS.md).

**Q50: How long does subsidy release take after application?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Each installment cycle typically takes 4-8 weeks from application to release. Delays may occur due to incomplete documentation, site inspection scheduling, or fund availability.

**Q51: Can I receive the full subsidy upfront?**

**OFFICIAL** — No, PMKSY subsidy is released in installments against certified expenditure and physical progress. Upfront release is not permitted.

See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md).

**Q52: What if there is a delay in grant release?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Submit complete documentation, maintain pre-inspection readiness, follow up weekly, and escalate through the implementing agency's grievance mechanism if delayed beyond normal timelines.

## Chocolate Manufacturing-Specific Questions

**Q69: Is chocolate manufacturing explicitly listed under PMKSY?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Chocolate manufacturing is not explicitly named in PMKSY public documents but falls under general "food processing" category. Component 2 (Creation/Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities) is the applicable component.

See [PMKS-001: Scheme Overview](PMKSY_MASTER/01_SCHEME_OVERVIEW.md) and [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm chocolate-specific treatment with MoFPI.

**Q70: What capacity is viable for a ₹2 crore chocolate unit?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Typical viable capacities for ₹2 crore investment: plain/milk chocolate 500 MT/year, dark chocolate 300 MT/year, compound chocolate 1,000 MT/year. Actual capacity depends on technology, market, and product mix.

See [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm PMKSY-specific capacity thresholds.

**Q71: Is cocoa butter or cocoa liquor import eligible?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Eligibility of imported raw materials (cocoa butter, cocoa liquor) for PMKSY subsidy depends on whether they are part of eligible processing infrastructure or working capital. Confirm with implementing agency.

See [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**Q72: What equipment is eligible for subsidy?**

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Eligible equipment includes: roasting machines, winnowing machines, ball mills/refiners, conche machines, tempering machines, moulding/enrobing lines, cooling tunnels, packaging machines, and auxiliary equipment. Energy-efficient and Industry 4.0 technologies may attract additional incentives.

See [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md) and [PMKS-006: Eligible Project Cost](PMKSY_MASTER/06_ELIGIBLE_PROJECT_COST.md).

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm equipment eligibility list with MoFPI/nodal agency.

**Q73: Are chocolate-specific quality certifications (BIS, ISO) required?**

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — BIS IS 11555 for chocolate may be voluntary or mandatory. ISO 22000, HACCP, and GMP/GHP are recommended but may not be mandatory. Verify with FSSAI/BIS.

See [PMKS-013: Compliance](PMKSY_MASTER/13_COMPLIANCE.md).
