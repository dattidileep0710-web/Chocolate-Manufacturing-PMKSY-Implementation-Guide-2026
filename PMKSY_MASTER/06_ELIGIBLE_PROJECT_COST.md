# Eligible Project Cost

## Purpose of Eligible Project Cost

This document defines which expenditure items qualify for PMKSY subsidy calculation. **Eligible Project Cost (EPC)** is the financial base on which subsidy percentages are applied. Only costs falling within approved categories and ceilings attract subsidy support.

**OFFICIAL** — PMKSY subsidy is computed as a percentage of Eligible Project Cost, not Total Project Cost. Understanding EPC boundaries is therefore critical for project financial planning and DPR preparation.

> **SCOPE BOUNDARY**: This document addresses **project cost eligibility** — what costs qualify for subsidy. It does **not** address:
> - Project eligibility criteria (see [PMKS-002: Project Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/02_PROJECT_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Applicant eligibility (see [PMKS-003: Applicant Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/03_APPLICANT_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Entity eligibility (see [PMKS-004: Entity Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/04_ENTITY_ELIGIBILITY.md))
> - Land eligibility (see [PMKS-005: Land Eligibility](PMKSY_MASTER/05_LAND_ELIGIBILITY.md))

## Key Concepts

### Eligible Project Cost (EPC)

**OFFICIAL** — EPC represents the capital expenditure items approved by the implementing agency for subsidy calculation. EPC is typically a subset of Total Project Cost (TPC).

| Term | Definition | Status |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Total Project Cost (TPC)** | Sum of all capital expenditures required to complete the project | OFFICIAL |
| **Eligible Project Cost (EPC)** | Portion of TPC that qualifies for PMKSY subsidy | OFFICIAL |
| **Ineligible Cost** | Portion of TPC that does not qualify for subsidy | OFFICIAL |
| **Promoter Contribution** | Equity/own funds invested by promoters | OFFICIAL |
| **Bank Term Loan** | Debt financing from banks/financial institutions | OFFICIAL |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Typical relationship for a ₹2 crore chocolate manufacturing project:

| Cost Category | Approximate Share | Purpose |
|---------------|-------------------|---------|
| Total Project Cost (TPC) | 100% | Complete project cost including ineligible items |
| Eligible Project Cost (EPC) | 75-90% of TPC | Items approved for subsidy |
| Subsidy Base | EPC × subsidy % | Calculated subsidy amount |
| Promoter Contribution | 25-35% of TPC | Own funds (minimum per scheme) |
| Bank Term Loan | Balance | Debt financing |

### Why EPC Matters

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — EPC determines:
- Subsidy amount received (EPC × subsidy rate)
- Promoter contribution required (TPC − bank loan − subsidy)
- Bank appraisal quantum (based on TPC, not EPC)
- Project feasibility (if subsidy assumption is optimistic, project may fail)

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Always prepare financial models with conservative EPC assumptions. Do not rely on maximum subsidy rates during project planning.

## Ineligible Expenditure Categories

### Typically Ineligible Costs

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — The following are generally not included in EPC:

| Cost Category | Reason for Ineligibility |
|---------------|-------------------------|
| Working capital | Revenue/operational expense, not capital |
| Cost of existing assets | Not new investment |
| Fines and penalties | Not project expenditure |
| Interest on borrowed funds | Financial cost, not capital cost |
| Cost of land acquisition (often) | Excluded or capped by many schemes |
| Brokerage / commissions | Not productive asset |
| Preliminary expenses above ceiling | Overhead beyond limits |
| Personal equipment | Not project-related |
| Luxury fittings | Not productive asset |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm whether working capital margin, interest during construction, and land cost are excluded or capped under current PMKSY guidelines.

## Cost Estimation Methodology

### Bottom-Up Estimation

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Prepare EPC using bottom-up estimation:

| Step | Action | Output |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | List all equipment and materials | Bill of quantities |
| 2 | Obtain vendor quotations | Price estimates |
| 3 | Estimate civil works | Quantity × rate |
| 4 | Estimate utilities | Load-based sizing |
| 5 | Add pre-operative costs | Percentage of capital |
| 6 | Add contingencies | 5-10% of capital cost |
| 7 | Segregate eligible/ineligible | EPC vs. ineligible |
| 8 | Validate norms | Against MoFPI guidelines |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — For a ₹2 crore chocolate unit, typical cost breakup might be:

| Cost Component | % of TPC | % of EPC |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| Land | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Ineligible / capped |
| Civil works | 20-25% | Eligible (within limits) |
| Plant and machinery | 45-55% | Eligible |
| Electrical and utilities | 10-15% | Eligible |
| Lab equipment | 5-8% | Eligible |
| Pre-operative expenses | 3-5% | Eligible (within limits) |
| Contingencies | 5-10% | Eligible (within limits) |
| Working capital | [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION] | Ineligible |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — This breakup is illustrative only. Actual costs depend on project scale, technology choice, location, and current market rates.

## Relationship with Bank Appraisal

### Bank-Cost Interface

**BANK PRACTICE** — Banks appraise project costs independently of PMKSY:

| Bank Practice | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Independent cost verification | Bank-appointed valuer or consultant |
| Vendor quotation review | Price reasonableness |
| Physical verification | Site inspection before disbursement |
| Disbursement linked to EPC | Bank releases loan against eligible expenditure |

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Banks typically:
1. Appraise full TPC for term loan quantum
2. Verify each cost component independently
3. Disburse against actual expenditure (not upfront subsidy)
4. Coordinate with MoFPI for subsidy release

**BANK PRACTICE** — Bank term loan is typically released as:
- 5-10% as margin money (promoter contribution)
- 10-15% against order placement
- 40-50% against civil progress
- 20-30% against machinery supply
- Balance against installation and commissioning

### Subsidy-Bank Coordination on Costs

**BANK PRACTICE** — Banks and MoFPI coordinate on cost verification:
- Bank verifies expenditure before disbursing loan installment
- Implementing agency verifies expenditure before releasing subsidy
- Both may inspect site jointly
- Subsidy released only after bank confirms expenditure

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Cost disputes between bank and MoFPI are resolved in favor of the more stringent interpretation.

See [PMKS-010: Project Appraisal](PMKSY_MASTER/10_PROJECT_APPRAISAL.md) for bank appraisal procedures. See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md) for subsidy disbursement procedures.

## Cost Verification and Audit

### Pre-Sanction Verification

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Before project sanction:
- Implementing agency reviews DPR cost estimates
- Bank independently appraises costs
- MoFPI/Nodal agency may seek third-party technical opinion
- Costs justified through vendor quotations and market rates

### Post-Sanction Verification

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — During implementation:
- Bank releases loan against certified expenditure
- Implementing agency releases subsidy against certified expenditure
- Site inspections verify physical progress
- Original invoices and bills required

### Post-Completion Audit

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — After project completion:
- Final cost audit by chartered accountant
- Certificate of actual expenditure
- Comparison with sanctioned EPC
- Excess/short utilization explained

## Common Cost Eligibility Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Inflating Costs to Maximize Subsidy
- **Error**: Overstating equipment or civil costs
- **Prevention**: Use market rates; bank and MoFPI independently verify

### Mistake 2: Mixing Eligible and Ineligible Costs
- **Error**: Combining land purchase with land development costs
- **Prevention**: Segregate clearly; explain methodology

### Mistake 3: Underestimating Ineligible Costs
- **Error**: Planning assuming 100% subsidy coverage
- **Prevention**: Model financially with conservative subsidy assumptions

### Mistake 4: Missing Cost Ceilings
- **Error**: Exceeding scheme-prescribed limits per category
- **Prevention**: Review MoFPI cost norms before DPR finalization [REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION]

### Mistake 5: Inadequate Vendor Documentation
- **Error**: Proceeding without formal quotations
- **Prevention**: Obtain at least 3 vendor quotations for major equipment

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Contingency Realism
- **Error**: Zero or low contingency allocations
- **Prevention**: Allow 5-10% for unforeseen items

### Mistake 7: Working Capital Shortfall
- **Error**: Assuming PMKSY subsidy covers working capital
- **Prevention**: Plan working capital separately from EPC

## Cost Classification Decision Tree

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — Use this decision tree to classify any cost:

```
Is the expenditure for a tangible/intangible asset created for the project?
├── NO → Is it a pre-operative cost directly attributable to project setup?
│   ├── YES → Is it within prescribed pre-operative expense limits?
│   │   ├── YES → ELIGIBLE (within limits)
│   │   └── NO → INELIGIBLE (exceeds ceiling)
│   └── NO → Is it working capital / revenue expense?
│       ├── YES → INELIGIBLE (operating expense)
│       └── NO → Review specific MoFPI guidance
└── YES → Is it part of approved project scope?
    ├── NO → INELIGIBLE (out of scope)
    └── YES → Does it fall within category-specific ceilings?
        ├── YES → ELIGIBLE
        └── NO → INELIGIBLE (exceeds ceiling)
```

## Relationship with Subsidy Calculation

**PROJECT ASSUMPTION** — EPC directly determines subsidy:

| Parameter | Formula | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Subsidy Amount | EPC × Subsidy Rate % | Rate varies by area type |
| Maximum Subsidy | EPC × Max Rate % | Capped per scheme norms |
| Promoter Contribution | TPC − Bank Loan − Subsidy | Minimum applicable % |
| Bank Loan | TPC − Promoter Contribution | Adjusted for subsidy |

**REQUIRES RE-VERIFICATION** — Confirm exact subsidy rates, maximum ceilings, and stacking rules from current MoFPI guidelines.

**ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION** — Prepare three financial scenarios:
1. **Conservative**: Minimum subsidy rate; maximum own funds
2. **Base**: Expected subsidy rate; standard contribution
3. **Optimistic**: Maximum subsidy rate; minimum own funds

Plan for conservative scenario; surplus from optimistic scenario is bonus.

See [PMKS-011: Grant Release](PMKSY_MASTER/11_GRANT_RELEASE.md) for subsidy release mechanics.
